About Me

I used to trade and I made a lot of money pretty early. But I felt like an imposter and I didn't like who I was becoming. I spent most days staring at a screen, disconnected from people, purpose, and myself.

So I stopped, I walked away, and started over.

Now I'm rebuilding everything from scratch. One habit, one purpose, and one hard day at a time. I'm training Jiu Jitsu, leading Metriq, learning to market, teaching myself to think clearly, act with weight, and live with intent.

I do this for my family, for God, and for the man I'm becoming. I'm not where I want to be yet, but I know where I'm going.

My Story

This is the story of how I went from Seoul to the States, from rags to riches, and from chasing success to building purpose.

Chapter 1: Seoul â†’ Chicago

I was born in Seoul, South Korea, on August 1st. At 4, my family moved to Chicago, chasing a better future. We grew up in a small apartment, but my parents always made sure I was happy.

I didn't realize we were poor, not until a field day in elementary school. Everyone wore matching $15 jerseys. My mom grabbed a plain white shirt, wrote my name and number on it. I was extremely embarrassed back then, but now, I see it for what it was: love and sacrifice. A gesture that means everything to me now.

Chapter 2: The Spark

We moved to Atlanta when I was 13. That's where the hustle started.

At 13, I opened a Robinhood account under my dad's name. October 23rd, 2017, my first recorded trade. I used $300 of birthday money and dove into penny stocks. It didn't go great.

So I pivoted. Started reselling sneakers and streetwear in high school. By sophomore year, I'd made $30,000, enough to help pay part of my parents' down payment when we moved into a house in 2020.

I built Instagram sneaker pages with over 200,000 combined followers. Started a sneaker membership group that hit $3,500 MRR at age 15. Sold everything for $10,000 and walked away. I didn't love the work, but I learned how to move, how to market, and how to create value.

Chapter 3: Risk, Reward, and Reinvention

When COVID hit, crypto and NFTs blew up. I turned $5K into $500K, then back down to $25K when the market crashed.

Most people would've been destroyed by that, but I wasn't. I knew I could do it again. That's my edge: stupid optimism and unwavering self-belief.

I skipped college. Tried dropshipping and failed. Tried learning to code and hated it.

So I returned to what I knew: reselling. I didn't love it, but I scaled it hard, $3.7M in revenue between 2022 and early 2024. Then crypto came back and I went all in. I was able to turn $150K into $5.5M. I felt like every lesson from 2020 had paid off. Then I lost most of it again.

Chapter 4: The Mirror

At the start of 2025, I was down 80%. Still ahead and still blessed. But I knew I was repeating the same loop: chase, win, drift, repeat.

This time, I paused. Not to quit, but to change the why.

I started learning to build. Not just to make money, but to make something real. I started tracking my habits, journaling, reflecting, leading my first product, and learning how to market.

I'm still trading. But now I'm building, learning, and sharpening. I want to become someone who doesn't just spot opportunity, but creates it. Someone who adds weight to the world.
