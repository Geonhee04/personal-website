My Story

This is the story of how I went from Seoul to the States, from rags to riches, and from chasing success to building purpose.

Chapter 1: Seoul → Chicago

I was born in Seoul and moved to Chicago when I was four. We grew up in a small apartment, but my parents always made sure I was happy, even when we didn’t have much.

The first time I realized we were struggling was during a school field day. Everyone had matching $15 jerseys. I wore a plain white shirt with my name hand-written by my mom. I didn’t understand it then, but that moment stuck. It planted a seed. If I wanted more, I’d have to earn it.

Chapter 2: The Hustle Begins

We moved to Atlanta when I was 13. That's where the hustle started.

I opened my first Robinhood account at 13, using $300 of birthday money I'd saved up. I tried penny stocks, got wrecked, and so I pivoted.

Started reselling sneakers, streetwear and built IG pages with over 200,000 combined followers. By 15, I was making $3500 MRR from my membership group and helped my parents pay for our house.

I didn't love the work, but I learned how to spot opportunity early, how to market, and how to create value.

Chapter 3: Riding the waves

When COVID hit, crypto and NFTs blew up. I turned $5K into $500K, then back down to $25K when the market crashed.

Most people would’ve been wrecked, but I wasn’t. I knew I could do it again. That’s always been my greatest edge: stupid optimism and unwavering self-belief.

So I went back to what I knew. From 2022 to 2024, I scaled reselling hard, pulling in $3.8M in revenue. It wasn’t glamorous, but it taught me how to move fast, operate under pressure, and keep stacking when the game changed.

Then the markets came back and I went all in. Turned $150K into $5.5M. Every lesson from 2020 felt like it had finally paid off. And then I lost most of it again.

Chapter 4: The Mirror

At the start of 2025, I was down 80%. Still ahead and still blessed. But I knew I was repeating the same loop: chase, win, drift, repeat.

This time, I paused. Not to quit, but to change the why.

So I started building. Not just products, but myself.

I'm still trading. But now I'm building, learning, and sharpening. I want to become someone who doesn't just spot opportunity, but creates it. Someone who adds weight to the world.
